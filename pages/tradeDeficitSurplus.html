TRADE DEFICIT & SURPLUS

TRADE DEFICIT:-

A negative trade balance is also known as the trade deficit is said to take place when in a country the total value of the imported goods is higher than the value of all exported goods.

As of 2019 the countries with which India has the highest trade deficit (in US $)–

  1. China: -$51.2 billion
  2. Saudi Arabia: -$20.9 billion
  3. Iraq: -$19.6 billion
  4. Switzerland: -$16.4 billion
  5. South Korea: -$11.4 billion

Whenever there is a negative cash flow with a country it represents India’s competitive disadvantage with those nations, at the same time represent key opportunities for India to develop some country-specific plans and actions to strengthen its overall position in international trade.


TRADE SURPLUS:-

A negative trade balance is also known as the trade deficit is said to take place when in a country the total value of the imported goods is higher than the value of all exported goods.

As of 2019 the countries with which India has the highest trade surplus –

  1. United States: US$19.5 billion (country-specific trade surplus in 2019)
  2. Bangladesh: $7.1 billion
  3. Nepal: $6.3 billion
  4. Netherlands: $5.1 billion
  5. Sri Lanka: $3.3 billion

Whenever there is a positive cash flow with a country then it represents India’s competitive advantage with those nations.
