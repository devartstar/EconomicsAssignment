Solutions to India's Trade Deficit

There is no doubt that Indiaâ€™s trade deficit with China is part of a larger challenge of trade competitiveness, which consists of a range of issues from domestic macroeconomic imbalances to the failure to enter into global supply chains.
Some solutions on which India can work upon are -

  1. India has to rein in its domestic macroeconomic imbalance and on other hand build competitive industrial capabilities.
  2. The US-China trade war provides India with a great opportunity to mark its return in the global manufacturing market as the global supply chain gets rewired.
  3. Rising wages in China are also another factor that could work for India.
  4. One of the largest factors is that India is losing out to its regional peer countries such as Vietnam, Indonesia, Bangladesh, Malaysia, and the Philippines.
  5. India has to do larger global investment projects.
  6. The larger lesson is that such industrial projects should help India plug into global supply chains, and use them as conveyor belts for exports of manufactured goods.

India can also apply some improved quality norms and technical regulations to decrease the number of imports.
India has also applied some anti-dumping duties and countervailing duties on imported goods to control the number of imports.

Conclusion

The trend of a decrease in the trade deficit for the last few years and with India walking away from the RCEP pact may lead to a decrease in trade imbalance with China. Since China is the major cause of the trade deficit of India, making the Indian market more prominent in the Chinese Region and avoiding the use of Chinese goods can lead to better trading numbers between India and China. Making more strict regulations with respect to unacceptable trading practices of imports by applying the tariffs and duties such as anti-dumping and pushing the domestic market further towards specialization will eventually help in increasing the export count and will help to reduce the Negative Trade Balance or Trade Deficit of India in upcoming years.
