INDIA CHINA TRADE

Since China accounts for almost 1/3 rd of the trade deficit of India it is essential to focus our views on India-China Trade. India has such a large trade deficit with china because 14% of India’s imports come from China followed by 7.5% by the USA.

Such huge numbers raise questions in our mind, as to why does India has such a huge Negative Trade with China?

Firstly, according to macroeconomics, China enjoys a huge trade surplus as it saves more than it invests. China has been strongly interventionist in the way it manages exchange rates.
Secondly, China actively used subsidies to promote its domestic industry thus giving it unfair advantages in many ways (like the subsidies to the cost of production of manufactured goods).
Thirdly, There are fears of the Indian policy establishment about deep links between the Chinese Industrial Sector and Government in Beijing resulting in bilateral trade becoming a part of a larger geostrategic dilemma.

How the pandemic affected the India-China trade deficit -

India halves its trade gap with China
With the start of the new Financial Year FY21, within the first 5 months(ie.April to August) India saw a huge decrease in trade deficit with China to about 5.8 billion dollars resulting in a total trade deficit of 12.8 billion dollars. This is also being considered as the lowest that India has touched with China in a decade.

So why is there a decrease in the Trade Deficit suddenly this year -

  • It is due to more export form Indias side (About 27% increase)
  • It is due to a decrease in import from China (About 27% decrease)

Though India saw a rise in the number of exports to China but other top 5 trading partners of India saw a decrease in the exports -

  1. Exports to the USA are down by 24.6%
  2. Exports to the UAE are down by 59.2%
  3. Exports to Singapore are down by 26.7%

Is this narrowing of the trade gap with china Sustainable?

Economists beliefs that this narrowing of the trade gap may not be sustainable in the long run since in this current pandemic situation domestic demand is currently squeezed which explains the reason for lower imports.
Arpita Mukherjee (professor of Indian Council on International Economics Relation) said that there may be rerouting of the goods taking place. China may be exporting its goods to Hong Kong or Singapore and India are indirectly importing goods from them. I want to bring light to another factor that might have led to such a huge trade deficit between India and China.
India initially participated in RCEP negotiations but in 2019 India walked away from these negotiations.
