TRADE DEFICIT IN PANDEMIC

Discussion on Trade Deficit under the Cloud of Covid

India has been in trade deficits since the year 1980 which is due to the increase in imports.
WHAT HAPPENED IN THE MONTH OF JUNE 2020 WHEN INDIA REGISTERING TRADE SURPLUS AFTER 18 YEARS.

The COVID19 pandemic largely affected the exports and imports of all variety of goods all around the world.

  1. For the first time in over 18 years as the domestic demands decrease following the COVID19 pandemic which affected imports to decrease more than exports.
  2. In the month of June, merchandise export reduced by 12.4 %.
  3. Imports decreased by 47.6% leading to a trade surplus of $790 million

One of the main reasons for the decrease in the imports was the decrease in the demand for crude oil resulting in a decrease in import of crude oil by 55.3 % compared to that of July 2019.

What does the future hold In Post Pandemic Situation?

Due to the delayed recovery in imports in pandemic situation economists predict for the merchandise trade deficit to decrease to $12 billion in the first quarter of FY2021 as compared to $46 billion in the first quarter of FY2020.
Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) has estimated the world economy to contract by 7% in 2020. It also excepts India’s economy to contract by as much as 7.3% in the FY2021, if there is a second wave of coronavirus in the country.
