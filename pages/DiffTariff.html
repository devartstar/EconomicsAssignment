TARIFFS AND TAXES

RCEP ( Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership )

It pact aimed at free trade corridor between the members so that good and services can flow freely in between them. The scale of RCEP is aimed at making a game changer as it could have been the world’s largest trading pact with a 1/3rd of global GDP and half of the world population under it.

The main initiative was given by China even though RCEP is built around ASEAN and hence has major China bias in the deal. It was proposed by Beijing in 2012 in order to counter the trans-pacific partner agreement. But India soon chooses to walk away from RCEP. Experts were of the view that the trade barriers were lifted and the Indian market would have been flooded with Chinese goods thus killing Indian businesses.

India’s trade deficit with RCEP countries has been huge due to a reduction of 80% to 90% of goods would have meant a sweet deal for China but maybe an absolute disaster for India.

The sectors of the Indian market that would have felt are

  • Dairy sector
  • Textile sector
  • Marine
  • Chemical pharmaceuticals
  • Iron steel and non-ferrous metal

All this meant that jobs would have gone further destroyed and Indian small or middle producers would have faced the Chinese market.

So what was India seeking from it?

  • Keep Dairy, Auto, and certain textiles out of the pact
  • Options to stop imports or raise duties in case of sudden flooding of the market
  • Stricter investment regime and dispute management
  • Stricter ‘origin of product’ norms to curb abuse of FTA.
  • Change in base tariff from 2014 to 2019

Tariffs and Duties

TARIFFS AND DUTIES TO DECREASE ARE APPLIED TO REDUCE SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITIES AND IMPORTS The WTO( World Trade Organization ) imposes some duty on the trades and goods imported or exported so to help some of the middle and lower economic countries so that their domestic market is not destroyed by the developed countries.

  1. DUMPING

    It is said to take place when a company exports its product at a price that is lower than the price it normally charges in its nation. A country may do this maybe due to the increase in stock or something.

  2. ANTI-DUMPING DUTY

    It is the imposition of a levy by the importing country in order to counter the dumping of products and save the domestic market. Article 6 of the GATT allowed countries to take action against dumping. But in order to impose this duty, the government has to be able to show that dumping is taking place by calculating the extent of dumping and show that the dumping is causing damage.
    Example- India has recently imposed anti-dumping duty on 99 Chinese products on January 28.

  3. COUNTERVAILING DUTY (CVDs)

    It is also known as anti-subsidy duties. They are used to counter the negative impact of imports to protect the local producers. They are imposed after an investigation finds that a foreign country subsidizes its exports It is imposed in accordance with the GATT Article 6.
    Example- On September 9, 2019 – DGTR(Directorate General of Trade and Remedies) concluded that Saccharin has been exported to India at a subsidized value which resulted in the subsidization of the product.

  4. SAFEGUARD TARIFF

    It helps in restricting the imports of the products temporarily(cannot last more than 4 yrs but in certain conditions can be extended to 8 yrs) if its domestic industry is threatened by the surge in imports.
    Example- We are pushing a lot towards solar energy so As of 2018 about 90% of the Solar Panels was imported…so the safeguard tariff was applied for 2 yrs.
